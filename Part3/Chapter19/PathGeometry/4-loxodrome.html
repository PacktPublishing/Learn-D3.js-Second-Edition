Loxodrome and orthodrome

Demonstrating "straight lines" in different projections. The blue line (orthodrome) is a great circle - the shortest distance between Los Angeles and London, but it appears as a curve in regular cylindrical projections (Mercator, Natural Earth, Plate Carée, Equal Area).

If you use it for navigation, you need to adjust the bearing at every point. The red line (loxodrome), a straight line in Mercator, has a constant bearing, but it is a longer route. You can rotate the globe so that this specific line appears straight (press the rotate([60, -60, 0]) button. In the Gnomonic projection, all great circles (orthodromes) are straight lines, but loxodromes are not. The Gnomonic projection projects the shortest distance as a straight line.

To render the red line, it must be resampled so that its points are projected correctly on the map.
