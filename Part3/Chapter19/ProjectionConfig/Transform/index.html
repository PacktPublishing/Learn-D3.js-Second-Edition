Viewport translation, scaling and 2D rotation along geo center

Use these methods to transform a projected map. Translation receives pixel coordinates of where to place the geo center of the map. Center changes the geo center. Scale and angle respectively scale and rotate around that center. All methods are projection-dependent.

  • Default translation. Geo center (equator x prime meridian) at 240,480.
  • Translate to (0,0) pixels. Move geo center to top-left viewport position.
  • Translate to place Tokyo in center. Moves geo center down and left.
  • Translate to place India in center. Moves geo center down and left.
  • Default center. Geo center (equator x prime meridian) at 240,480.
  • Center Tokyo. Center changed to Tokyo coordinates.
  • Center India. Center changed to India centroid.
  • Default scale for the Mercator projection.
  • Zoom out scale 75. Fits complete Mercator projection
  • Zoom in scale 160. Slightly more than Mercator default.
  • Zoom in 480. Applying scale(480).
  • Zoom in 1000. Applying scale(1000).
  • Center-Tokyo with scale. Tokyo at center and zoomed in.
  • Center-India with scale. india at center and zoomed in.
  • Center-Tokyo with scale and translate. Geo center + viewport transforms.
  • Rotate Mercator by 45 deg.
  • Rotate Mercator by 90 deg.
  • Rotate Orthographic projection by 45 deg.
  • Center-India with scale, angle and translate. Geo center + viewport transforms.
