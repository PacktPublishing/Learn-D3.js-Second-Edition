Dragging, rotating, and zooming a map in D3

This implementation applies a naive approach to interactive rotation using mouse coordinates. You can zoom in and out and rotate the map by dragging the mouse. All effects are implemented using d3.zoom and euler angles (yaw, pitch, roll). The implementation, however, assumes a globe in the conventional position where the north is above, and the south is below. If you rotate the map by 90 degrees, the rotation will be applied in the wrong direction. The other examples in this folder use quaternions via an external library.
